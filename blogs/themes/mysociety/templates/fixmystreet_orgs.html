FixMyStreet

Shift your problem reporting online

Local authorities everywhere are looking to move street issue reporting online, in order to deliver savings and improve customer satisfaction. FixMyStreet for Councils is a beautiful, cloud hosted street issue reporting application designed specifically for local authority needs.

FixMyStreet for Councils helps you reduce costs by making fault reporting into a self-service experience, thus eliminating manual data entry. It also helps reduce duplicate reporting by showing problems already known to the council. As a mature cloud-based service, it is cost-effective, highly customisable and simple to implement.

Based on the UK's most widely used problem-reporting website, FixMyStreet for Councils prioritises usability and accessibility – making it a service that people love, rather than tolerate.

Case Studies:

  • London Borough of Barnet

    Barnet Council have used FixMyStreet software on their website since January 2010, and have more recently integrated it with a SAP CRM system and enabled mobile reporting. The service has had a great impact: "It's an entirely positive relationship with a resident. A resident has seen something in the street isn’t working, they inform the council and we’ll go and fix it. It rather changes our view of residents - it makes them our eyes and ears on the ground." - Chris Palmer, Assistant Director of Communications, Barnet Borough Council.

  • London Borough of Bromley

    London Borough of Bromley is the latest local authority to take advantage of FixMyStreet for Councils on their website. We also integrated our software with Bromley’s in-house back-office system for managing faults using the Open311 protocol. As a result, issues reported on FixMyStreet go straight into Bromley’s systems without manual re-keying. When a status of an issue changes - for example, from “Investigating” to “In progress” this is automatically reflected on Bromley’s homepage as well as the national FixMyStreet.com website. A fully automated link with no manual integration means that the council can save time and resources in processing street issue reports.
